Since the first batch

One cookie, two boroughs of opinion.

The black & white cookie has been a New York argument for a hundred years — cake or cookie, fudge or fondant, which half do you bite first. We didn't try to settle it. We built a whole shop around the disagreement.

Every flavor we make keeps the same rule the original did: half is bold, half is quiet, and the line down the middle is the whole point. Vanilla meets cocoa. Sweet meets bitter. Brooklyn meets the rest of the city.

The lineup

Five halves, five arguments.

Each flavor is split right down the center — never blended, never compromised. Pick a side, or order all five and stay out of it.

The Original

Vanilla cake, half dipped in dark chocolate fondant, half in vanilla. The argument starter.

02

Red Velvet Divide

Cocoa-red cake on one side, cream cheese fondant on the other. Loud and proud.

03

Cinnamon Babka

Swirled cinnamon-sugar cake, half glazed dark, half left golden. A Brooklyn bakery favorite.

04

Pistachio Park

Toasted pistachio cake split between deep olive and pale green fondant. Quietly the boldest one.

05

Lemon Borough

Bright lemon cake, half in citrus glaze, half in vanilla. Built for a hot sidewalk.
